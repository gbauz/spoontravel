## The Experience

Artemis II marks the return of human spaceflight around the Moon after more than five decades. Its splashdown in the Pacific Ocean is not just a technical milestone — it’s a global moment.

For travelers, this kind of event represents a new category of experience: **being present during history**. Just like people travel to see eclipses or the Northern Lights, space missions are becoming part of the modern travel landscape.

## When It Happens

The Artemis II splashdown is expected at:

- **5:07 PM (Pacific Time)**
- **8:07 PM (Eastern Time)**

The landing zone is located off the coast of Southern California, near San Diego.

## Where to Watch

You don’t need a ticket to be part of it:

- Watch the official NASA livestream for full coverage.
- Follow live updates from global media.
- If you are in California, the coastal atmosphere adds a unique sense of proximity to the event.

## Why It Matters for Travel

This is more than a space mission — it’s a signal of what’s coming next.

- Space tourism is moving from concept to reality.
- Destinations linked to space exploration are gaining attention.
- The idea of travel is expanding beyond Earth.

What feels extraordinary today may become part of future itineraries.
